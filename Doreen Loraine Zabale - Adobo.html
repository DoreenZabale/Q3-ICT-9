Pork Adobo

Pork adobo is a popular Filipino dish and cooking method in the Philippines. Adobo from the Philippines is typically salty, sour, and sweet.

Pork Adobo Vs. Chicken Adobo

Pork belly is used in pork adobo, whereas chicken adobo can be made with any part of the chicken, including the thigh, drumsticks, and breasts. Both have the same flavor, but chicken adobo requires more ingredients than pork adobo.

Recipe versions of Adobo

Adobong Pusit - Adobong Pusit is a tasty squid dish prepared in the traditional Filipino adobo style.

Adobong Balut - Adobong Balut Recipe will help to brighten up the traditional Pinoy balut and transform it into a dish that is still very Filipino, which is Adobo.

Chicken Adobo - Chicken Adobo is a type of chicken stew from the Philippines. Chicken is marinated in soy sauce and spices before being pan-fried and stewed until tender.

Pork Adobo Recipe

Pork Adobo is a dish made of pork slices cooked in soy sauce, vinegar, and garlic. Adobo is a popular and well-liked dish in the Philippines.

Cuisine: Filipino

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly
  • 2 tablespoons garlic minced or crushed
  • 5 dried bay leaves
  • 4 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon peppercorn
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Marinate the pork belly for at least 1 hour in a mixture of soy sauce and garlic.
  2. Heat the pot and add the marinated pork belly then cook for a few minutes.
  3. Pour in the remaining marinade, including the garlic.
  4. Add the water, whole pepper corn, and dried bay leaves to a boil. Cook for 40 minutes to an hour.

  6. Season with salt to taste.
